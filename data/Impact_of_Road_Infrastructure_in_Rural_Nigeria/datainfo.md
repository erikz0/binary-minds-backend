# Title: Impact of Road Infrastructure in Rural Nigeria

## Description:
This dataset examines the impact of road infrastructure on various socio-economic factors in rural Nigeria. It includes data collected from 1233 respondents across different states and zones in Nigeria, focusing on aspects such as agricultural income, access to credit, household size, and asset ownership. The dataset aims to provide insights into how road infrastructure influences the livelihoods and economic activities of rural communities.

## Structure:
The dataset contains the following columns with their potential values:

- **id**: Unique identifier (int64)
- **year**: Year of data collection (2019, 2022)
- **uniqueid**: Unique identifier for respondents (object)
- **beneficiary_status**: Status of being a beneficiary or non-beneficiary (beneficiary, non-beneficiary)
- **bl_state**: State of residence (anambra, benue, ebonyi, enugu, kogi, niger, nassarawa, nasarawa, ogun, taraba)
- **zone**: Geographical zone (se, nc, sw, ne)
- **agerange**: Age range of respondents (36-45, 24-35, above 55, 46-55, 18-23)
- **gender**: Gender of respondents (1 for male, 0 for female)
- **hhsize**: Household size (float64)
- **nature_access_credit**: Nature of access to credit (formal, informal, non___formal, both, qur_anic)
- **access_credit**: Access to credit (yes, no, both)
- **maritalstatus**: Marital status (married, widowed, single, divorced)
- **bl_education**: Baseline education status (1 for educated, 0 for not educated)
- **croppingsystem_intercropping**: Practice of intercropping (yes, no)
- **croppingsystem_croprotation**: Practice of crop rotation (yes, no)
- **others_croppingsys**: Other cropping systems (yes, no)
- **formaleducation**: Level of formal education (secondary, post-secondary, university, primary)

## Context:
The dataset was collected to understand the socio-economic impact of road infrastructure development in rural Nigeria. It covers various states and zones, providing a comprehensive view of how improved road infrastructure can affect agricultural productivity, income levels, access to credit, and overall livelihood.
